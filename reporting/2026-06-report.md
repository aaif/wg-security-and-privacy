# [Report] Security & Privacy – June 2026

**Working Group:** WG Security & Privacy

**Reporting period:** June 2026

**Chair(s):** Alexander Frazer (Runlayer) & Junjie Bu (Google)

**Date submitted:** 2026-06-30

---

## Summary

In June the working group moved from formation into execution. Organizing the
workstreams and defining participation took longer than expected, largely because
outside demands left less time for the group's work over the past month. The AAIF made
the WG repositories public, and five workstreams were established with confirmed leads
and a new Workstreams folder. The Taxonomy effort merged into the cross-WG AAIF
Taxonomy/Landscape initiative, and the Threat Modeling and Best Practices workstreams
produced their first drafted content.

## Progress Against Objectives

- The Taxonomy of Terms is now the cross-WG AAIF Taxonomy/Landscape workstream and is no
  longer managed by this working group. Jay White and Katherine Druckman represent
  Security and Privacy as delegates.
- The Agentic AI Threat Modeling Gap Analysis advanced toward its 2026-07-01 target. A
  first draft was presented and the group endorsed its direction and depth.
- The Agentic AI Security Best Practices Guide has initial content drafted.
- All five workstreams now have confirmed leads and documented scope through
  per-workstream READMEs in the new Workstreams folder.
- Deliverable target dates for the active workstreams have been pushed back to reflect
  realistic pacing, since getting organized and started ran slower than expected due to
  outside impacts.

## Blockers and Risks

- General availability is a standing risk. Outside demands on members and leads can
  reduce the time they give the group and affect pace in unpredictable ways.

## Decisions Needed from the TC

- What level of collaboration and coordination should the working group pursue with
  external groups such as OWASP, AARM, and MCP ACS? The chairs see this as an
  opportunity for influence and consolidated messaging across the ecosystem, and request
  the TC's guidance on the scope and posture of these engagements.

## Next Month's Focus

1. Review and finalize the Agentic AI Threat Modeling Gap Analysis and commit the drafted
   Threat Modeling and Best Practices content to the repository.
2. Establish a steady meeting cadence and grow active contributor participation across
   the workstreams, prioritizing contributor recruitment.

## Metrics / Links

Status against the charter Success Metrics (KPIs):

- Adoption (references in talks, projects, social media): none tracked this period.
- Quality (alignment to deliverable standards): deliverables remain in draft; the Threat
  Modeling Gap Analysis draft was reviewed and endorsed for format and depth.
- Community (average member participation): meeting attendance is stable at about 15% of
  invited members, around 27 attendees per meeting. Leads and contributor rosters are
  confirmed across all five workstreams.
- Timeliness (milestones delivered on schedule): two June deliverables are pushed back into
  July based on the pace of ramping up the workstreams, and the Threat Modeling Gap
  Analysis remains on track for first milestone in July.
