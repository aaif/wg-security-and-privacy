## Topics Discussed

- **Co-chair introduction** — Junjie Bu (Google) introduced as the new co-chair of the working group.
- **Meeting time change** — Junjie presented Doodle poll results: Tuesday 8–9 am PST (10 votes), Tuesday 10–11 am PST (9 votes), and Wednesday 8–9 am PST (9 votes). Matt Khouzam suggested alternating times but the group flagged the risk of creating silos. Jeff Borek cautioned that shifting could collide with other AAIF working groups. Bar Kaduri noted overlap with the OWASP GenAI meeting at the current time. The group decided to run a narrowed follow-up poll.
- **Mission statement direction** — Alex presented five draft options: ecosystem-lens, builder-centered, and trust-centered. Lindsay Walker and Ofek Dadush favored trust-centered, arguing the group should focus on open frameworks and evaluation rather than prescribing specific standards. Madhura Raj cautioned that a purely trust-centered statement may miss the developer audience and keywords like "design patterns" and "best practices." Cosmin Radu proposed a hybrid framing such as "enabling builders to deliver trusted systems." Ty suggested replacing "shared vocabulary" with "shared taxonomy." Govindaraj advocated for a hybrid approach that accounts for the shared responsibility model between service providers and consumers. Alex took an action to draft new permutations incorporating the feedback for async review.
- **Scope proposals** — Alex walked through proposed in-scope and out-of-scope items. Govindaraj noted that agent identity and authorization overlaps with the Identity and Trust WG and suggested providing security/privacy requirements to that group rather than duplicating work. Raman Sethi raised runtime monitoring and incident response — kill switches, anomaly detection, and incident playbooks for agent failures — as a potential addition. Govindaraj suggested coordinating with the Observability and Tracing WG on behavioral anomalies. Youakim Badr asked whether risk management and threat assessment are covered. Alex encouraged everyone to add scope suggestions to the Google Doc for async refinement.
- **Cross-workgroup coordination** — Matt White confirmed a chairs meeting will be set up for cross-group collaboration. Alex described the cross-workgroup security and privacy review checklist concept as a way to share security/privacy requirements with other WGs.
- **Deliverables overview** — Alex presented five potential deliverables for discussion: (1) taxonomy of terms and definitions (already in progress), (2) agentic AI threat model framework, (3) security and privacy design patterns, (4) agentic AI security best practices guide, and (5) cross-workgroup security and privacy review checklist. Madhura asked whether the group should build on existing frameworks like Maestro (CSA) or create new ones. Govindaraj suggested contributing PRs to existing repos like Maestro rather than creating parallel frameworks. Matt White advised focusing on real unsolved problems rather than endorsing or promoting external initiatives. Sam Betts suggested a curated landing page of recommended frameworks could be useful for builders.
- **Taxonomy review** — Alex briefly reviewed the taxonomy, describing it as a living document that will be versioned over time. Encouraged all participants to add terms, flag disagreements, and suggest missing definitions.

## Decisions

- A narrowed follow-up poll will be conducted to finalize the meeting time change.
- The Google Doc remains the malleable working space; only finalized decisions will be recorded in the GitHub repo with dates and annotations.
- The group will provide security and privacy requirements to other WGs (Identity and Trust, Observability and Tracing) rather than duplicating their scope.

## Deliverables

- Taxonomy of terms and definitions remains the first deliverable, already in progress.
- Four additional potential deliverables identified for further discussion: threat model framework, design patterns, best practices guide, and cross-workgroup review checklist.
