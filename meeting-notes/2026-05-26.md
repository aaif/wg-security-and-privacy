## Topics Discussed

- **Charter officially approved** — The WG charter is now formally approved, making the group fully legitimate. Alex framed it as the moment to shift from celebrating to doing the real work.
- **Taxonomy and Landscape leadership** — Junjie Bu was appointed co-chair of the cross-WG Taxonomy workstream and chair of the Landscape workstream (surveying ecosystem tools and frameworks). The sign-up form is open; involvement is lightweight (contribute via PRs), with a weekly 30-minute meeting and most work done asynchronously. Matthew praised the taxonomy site but noted it skews toward commercial offerings and is light on definitions.
- **Discord vs. Slack platform question** — AAIF has an open question (raised by Manik) about whether to stay on Discord or move to Slack. Alex leans slightly toward Slack but has no hard preference; no major red flags were raised. Matthew suggested exploring open-source options instead (Mattermost, then Matrix), noting other SDOs are reportedly moving to Mattermost and that self-hosting keeps data under the group's control (vs. Slack hiding history past 90 days on the free tier).
- **Leadership structure / self-selection** — Rather than locking in the survey's leadership volunteers (Matthew and Govindaraj each volunteered for multiple roles), Alex will consolidate everyone into contributors per workstream and let each channel self-select leads and co-leads. Lead expectations are intentionally light: keep conversation moving, own status updates at the bi-weekly meetings, and delegate work across contributors.
- **Workstream staffing** — Best Practices Guide drew the most interest (large scope, easily subdivided). Design Patterns Catalog had fewer volunteers and may need scope/timeline adjustment. Threat Modeling has ~11 interested people and is well-staffed; it will overlap closely with Junjie's Taxonomy/Landscape work, letting the WG apply a security/privacy lens to shared data.
- **New members** — Fernando Lucktemberg (invited by an SAP colleague; finalizing a Creative Commons book on the agentic AI security stack), Aileen (TELUS), and Daniel King (Lancaster University) introduced themselves and offered to help.
- **GitHub repo access** — Katherine Druckman (JetBrains) and Fernando reported 404s / missing access. The group noted GitHub invites arrive by email (often landing in spam) and must be accepted; Christina at AAIF can help if the invite can't be found.
- **Deliverable timelines** — With a deliverable due the following week, Alex agreed to push deliverable due dates back by ~one month given the charter approval delays, and to share the updated timeline with leadership.
- **News You Can Use** — Wide-ranging tooling discussion: Gemini CLI's recent addition of plans (and Anti-Gravity), rising LLM/token costs, and running large open-source models locally (e.g., a 1T-parameter Qwen model on Intel Optane; used Tesla V100 32GB cards; DGX Spark / AMD boxes; air- vs. water-cooling). Max raised the idea of the WG acting as an independent open-source evaluator running models against security benchmarks. Fernando is publishing a series on building your own AI vulnerability scanner.
- **Information sharing in Discord** — The group agreed members should post links and resources directly in Discord (with tags for searchability) rather than relying on Alex to copy them from transcripts, both to preserve attribution and to enable follow-up conversation. Alex favors separate channels for social/link-sharing vs. group/organizational updates so management messages don't get flooded out.

## Decisions

- Survey leadership volunteers will be consolidated into contributors; leads and co-leads will be self-selected within each workstream's Discord channel rather than assigned from the survey.
- Deliverable due dates will be pushed back by approximately one month due to the charter approval delays.
- The group is generally (but not strongly) in favor of Slack over Discord and will not be a blocker if AAIF chooses to switch; feedback to be relayed to Manik.
