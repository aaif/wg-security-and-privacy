## Topics Discussed

- **Chair introduction** — Alex Frazer was elected chair and shared his background in AI security (Runlayer, Radiant Security, Cyber Reason/EDR). Requested volunteers for co-chair.
- **Meeting logistics** — Current time conflicts with the OWASP Agentic meeting (Bar Kaduri). Group agreed to send a Doodle poll via Discord to find a better slot while avoiding collisions with other AAIF working groups. Bi-weekly cadence maintained during charter drafting.
- **Tooling** — Default tooling is Discord, Google Docs, and GitHub. Alex invited suggestions for alternatives (e.g., Notion) but no decisions made.
- **Working group next steps** — Three milestones outlined: (1) finalize mission and scope, (2) determine deliverables, (3) finalize and submit charter to the governing board.
- **Charter themes review** — Alex organized the brainstorm from the previous meeting into themes using an LLM and walked through them. The group refined and expanded several themes.
- **Cross-working-group collaboration** — Ty proposed that this group's role may be to bring a security and privacy lens to the deliverables of other working groups (Identity and Trust, Data Protection, etc.). Alex and others agreed security and privacy is an intersecting layer across all groups.
- **Privacy and protected data** — Tom Sheffler raised the need for more privacy-focused content, including identifying types of protected data (e.g., HIPAA) and whether AI agents introduce new categories. Group agreed to expand Theme D to cover this.
- **Private agentic workflows** — Ty described privacy-preserving execution patterns where agents can access encrypted data but are prevented from leaking it externally (e.g., ZK proofs returning only boolean results). Tony Douglas (DRC) supported this, citing work on TEEs and encrypted vector databases. Group discussed adding "security/privacy" labeling to Theme D.
- **Secrets management and terminology** — Ofek Dadush raised secrets management. The group debated whether "secrets" covers only traditional credentials (API keys, env vars) or also confidential training data. David Deng suggested using "sensitive data" to avoid ambiguity. Samantha Coyle noted proprietary considerations should be included.
- **Shared taxonomy** — Matt White emphasized the need for a shared taxonomy of terms within the group, which would later bubble up across all AAIF working groups. The group agreed to start defining terms in the working document's taxonomy section.
- **Commerce and payments guardrails** — Lindsay Walker argued for keeping Theme H in scope, narrowed to security guardrails for commerce (preventing fund draining, account leaks, scope limits on payment agents).
- **Responsible AI / ethics** — Ofek asked whether bias, business ethics, and responsible AI are in scope. Jeff Borek recommended deferring ethics and value-judgment questions to the Governance and Policy Working Group. The group agreed.
- **Hallucinations and reliability** — Initially proposed as out-of-scope (deferred to the Accuracy and Reliability WG), but Jonas Pfoh and Nesreen Ahmed raised edge cases around non-malicious agent misbehavior and agent derailment/sabotage. Alex struck through the out-of-scope designation pending further discussion.
- **Rogue agent blast radius** — Samantha Coyle added considerations about protecting data from rogue agents and measuring blast radius to Theme D.

## Decisions

- Ethics, morality, and value-judgment questions are deferred to the Governance and Policy Working Group.
- Hallucinations/accuracy/reliability initially considered out-of-scope but not finalized; the group needs further discussion on guardrails for non-malicious agent misbehavior.
- The group will develop a shared taxonomy of terms internally before bubbling definitions up to the broader AAIF.
- Theme D expanded to include PII obfuscation, secrets management, privacy-preserving agentic workflows, and rogue agent data protection.
- Commerce (Theme H) kept in scope, narrowed to security guardrails.
