## Topics Discussed

- **Chair updates and LFX tooling** — Alex shared that the chairs received a demo of a new LFX app with more self-service control over meeting scheduling and meeting metadata. This should help workstream leads schedule meetings and eventually allow agendas to live directly in LFX meeting records.
- **Technical Committee update process** — Chairs are exploring an asynchronous monthly update flow through GitHub. The goal is to make WG progress visible to the Technical Committee for review and questions without turning updates into approval gates.
- **Workstream lead voting results** — The workstream co-lead vote had very low turnout, with only three voters and several one-vote ties. Alex walked through a proposed slate that tried to de-conflict duplicate leadership assignments while still honoring stated interest.
- **Taxonomy and Landscape workstream** — Junjie Bu described Taxonomy/Landscape as a cross-cutting AAIF workstream intended to build common terminology, resolve conflicting definitions, and maintain decoupled data that can be updated through PRs. Contributions are open to everyone, but each working group is asked to provide delegates who actively review and help resolve terminology questions.
- **Security and Privacy taxonomy delegates** — Jay White and Katherine Druckman volunteered to represent the Security and Privacy WG in the Taxonomy/Landscape workstream. Matthew Khouzam also expressed interest in participating where scheduling allows.
- **Workstream leadership assignments** — The group discussed the proposed workstream lead slate and confirmed most roles live on the call. Govindaraj Palanisamy will lead the Cross-WG Security and Privacy Review Checklist; Matthew Khouzam and Fernando Lucktemberg will co-lead the Best Practices Guide; Alon Mazor and Fernando Lucktemberg will co-lead Threat Modeling; Dan Kommatas and Sagar Dashora are proposed for the Design Patterns Catalog, with Dan to be confirmed offline.
- **Workstream membership and channels** — Sagar asked how contributors will be assigned. Alex will share the original interest-form lists with leads, who can re-engage interested contributors, recruit additional help, and choose whether to use the dedicated Discord channels or a more open communication model.
- **News You Can Use** — Members discussed recent AI/security items, including reports of prompt-injected commercial chatbots being used for coding, a Meta/Instagram account takeover issue, Mythos release expectations, MCP signing and tool attestation patches, Agent Gateway, and questions about how to get traction on MCP protocol changes.

## Decisions

- Jay White and Katherine Druckman will serve as Security and Privacy WG delegates to the cross-WG Taxonomy/Landscape workstream.
- Govindaraj Palanisamy will lead the Cross-WG Security and Privacy Review Checklist workstream.
- Matthew Khouzam and Fernando Lucktemberg will co-lead the Agentic AI Security Best Practices Guide workstream.
- Alon Mazor and Fernando Lucktemberg will co-lead the Agentic AI Threat Modeling Gap Analysis and Framework Design workstream.
- Sagar Dashora will co-lead the Security and Privacy Design Patterns Catalog workstream with Dan Kommatas, pending Alex's offline confirmation with Dan.
- Workstream leads will manage contributor engagement from the original interest-form lists and may recruit additional members as needed.
