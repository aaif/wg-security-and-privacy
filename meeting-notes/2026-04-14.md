## Topics Discussed

- **MCP Dev Summit updates — Interceptors** — Alex attended the MCP Dev Summit and highlighted the Interceptors proposal, a spec-level mechanism to standardize hooks across MCP implementations. Interceptors could help delegate security functions to non-security companies building agentic frameworks (e.g., allowing platforms like Cursor to plug in security capabilities without building them in-house). Alex has joined the Interceptors working group and will share the Discord link and details on the mailing list.
- **MCP Dev Summit updates — MCP Apps** — Alex flagged MCP Apps as a second area of interest, describing it as re-envisioning browser-like functionality for agents (e.g., verifying site identity and secure communication within agent conversations). He sees potential security and privacy concerns that are not yet widely discussed and suggested extending the group's cross-WG collaboration to cover this area.
- **Project Glasswing** — Alex briefly mentioned Project Glasswing under the Linux Foundation as worth monitoring for developments applicable to the group's work, even though AI-as-a-security-tool is somewhat outside the current scope.
- **Scope formal vote** — Alex presented the results of the informal scope vote (6 responses, all yes) and called a formal vote during the meeting. The scope was approved unanimously with over 15 green-check reactions and no objections. Sagar asked about post-incident forensics under scope item C (Agent Control and Containment); Alex clarified that scope defines the group's purview but does not commit to specific work — that commitment happens at the deliverable level.
- **Deliverables review and vote** — Alex walked through five proposed deliverables for the initial 3–6 month horizon, emphasizing that these are the first set and will be revisited as the landscape evolves. Each deliverable was discussed individually before calling a single vote to approve all five. The vote passed unanimously.
  - **Taxonomy** — A living reference document defining terms to be incorporated into the broader AAIF taxonomy. Considered straightforward and non-controversial.
  - **Agentic AI Threat Modeling Gap Analysis and Framework Design** — A research-oriented deliverable to assess whether there is room for a new framework or if the landscape is already well covered. Allie expressed interest in leading, citing her work on reinventing the "lethal trifecta" concept to address the complexity of modern threat contexts (internal/external threats, trust, identity, shared memory stores). Max raised future threat modeling for plastic neural networks that change weights during learning, creating new attack vectors. Ofek noted the group should find a genuinely new perspective rather than duplicating OWASP or NIST approaches.
  - **Security and Privacy Design Patterns Catalog** — The most technically oriented deliverable, targeting builders with patterns for human-in-the-loop, hooks/extensibility, enforcing logical or semantic constraints, and privacy-preserving execution.
  - **Agentic Security Best Practices Guide** — Practical, educational guidance one level above design patterns — covering topics like secrets management and secure tool invocation for builders new to agentic security. Allie noted this deliverable should work closely with the threat modeling deliverable.
  - **Cross-Working Group Review Checklist** — A tool to help other working groups recognize when their work has security and privacy implications and to engage the SP WG early, avoiding late-stage surprises. Philipp drew on IETF experience and endorsed the model, suggesting the group should eventually consider providing a dedicated review service beyond a checklist.
- **Deliverable leadership** — Govindaraj volunteered to lead either the Agentic Security Best Practices Guide or the Cross-Working Group Review Checklist. Alex noted that formal assignment will follow TC approval, and he will send a Google Form for volunteers.
- **Charter approval vote** — Alex walked through the remaining charter sections (operating model, meeting cadence, communication channels, roles, decision-making) and called a formal vote. The charter was approved unanimously.
- **Technical Committee presentation** — Jay reminded the group that the TC must approve the charter before teams can be formally assigned to deliverables. Alex confirmed the presentation to the TC is scheduled for the following week (Monday/Tuesday) and that he and Junjie will present the purpose, mission, scope, goals, and deliverables.
- **Communication channels** — Govindaraj asked about joining Discord/Slack. Alex confirmed Slack is now synced with Discord and directed him to email operations@AAIF (CC Alex) for a Slack invite.

## Decisions

- Working group scope approved unanimously via formal vote.
- Five initial deliverables approved unanimously for the 3–6 month horizon: (1) Taxonomy, (2) Agentic AI Threat Modeling Gap Analysis and Framework Design, (3) Security and Privacy Design Patterns Catalog, (4) Agentic Security Best Practices Guide, and (5) Cross-Working Group Review Checklist.
- Working group charter approved unanimously via formal vote.
