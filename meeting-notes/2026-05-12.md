## Topics Discussed

- **Charter approval status** — Voting has moved from GitHub to LFX due to technical issues with the original GitHub voting process. The charter is not yet officially approved, but a second round of feedback came back from David as positive confirmation. Alex is operating as if the charter is approved and will share an update once it is formal. Junjie asked where LFX voting can be checked; Alex is not yet sure whether the voting feature is exposed in the LFX platform.
- **LFX platform and MCP** — Alex met with Kieran from the Linux Foundation about the LFX platform. The platform has significant administrative tooling (including an MCP server in development) that working groups do not yet have access to; the LF MCP should make it much easier to track Discords, meetings, and updates across AAIF.
- **PR feedback on the charter** — Feedback on the charter PR has been minimal — the main note from David was a sentence Alex had over-relied on AI to draft, which has been reworked. The group treated the silence as confirmation that there are no further substantive concerns.
- **Meeting time and cadence** — The chairs discussed Manik's time-zone constraints (Sydney). Alex pushed back on rescheduling the WG meeting because ~82% of respondents are in Central European through Pacific time and accommodating Sydney would cut off both endpoints. The plan as workstreams form is to shorten the all-hands to ~30 minutes and consider going weekly with alternating time-zone-friendly slots, so the bi-weekly cadence is preserved for everyone.
- **Anonymous feedback form** — Alex will send an anonymous Google Form (built by the LF) once it is ready, to give members another channel for feedback — particularly important as the group moves into the deliverable phase. Goal is "fail fast": surface issues early so they can be addressed quickly.
- **Updates from the world ("News You Can Use")** — Erin shared recent CISA publications that align with several workstreams and encouraged the group to mine existing public work where it's relevant. Alex echoed that the field moves too fast for any one person to track and that collective surfacing of resources is the best approach. Erin's "News You Can Use" naming was adopted for this recurring agenda item. The recent supply-chain attack patched on Tuesday was also noted.
- **MCP Apps coordination** — Alex attended the MCP Apps meeting; security content was light (mostly basics like CSRF). He will get items onto the MCP Apps agenda before the next meeting to drive a security-focused conversation. Matthew noted he was already on the MCP Apps queue but they ran out of time, and that the queue is processed progressively.
- **AARM standard outreach** — Alex has a meeting with AARM (a standard developed by Vanta) as part of broader outreach to align with adjacent standards bodies. The goal is to build industry-wide alignment so the WG has more leverage when bringing recommendations to companies like OpenAI and Anthropic.
- **MCP ACS spec / hooks collaboration** — Alex mentioned an upcoming meeting with the MCP ACS spec group. Bar Kaduri clarified that the effort is broader than ACS — they are building a generalized hooks mechanism that anyone can implement. Bar co-leads one of the workstreams with Ariel; Alex is meeting with Ariel the next day, creating multiple collaboration touchpoints.
- **Deliverable interest survey results** — The survey received 15 responses, which Alex flagged as light given typical attendance is 20–30. He's holding off on confirming workstream leadership until more responses come in. Threat Modeling / Gap Analysis and the Cross-WG Security & Privacy Review Checklist still need leadership volunteers.
- **Taxonomy elevated to cross-WG initiative** — Taxonomy is now an AAIF-wide cross-WG effort. Leadership selection is being run by Manik and is expected to close this week (Junjie noted the vote was set to close that day). Junjie is a likely lead candidate. SP WG members who volunteered for taxonomy will participate as contributors into the larger cross-WG effort and bring the existing SP WG draft as a starting point.
- **Workstream organization model** — Each workstream will get its own Discord channel for focused async coordination. Workstream leaders will self-organize meeting times and cadence; the only requirement is providing status updates during the bi-weekly (or future weekly) all-hands. Where two or more people volunteered for leadership, Alex will facilitate selection of co-leads to share the load.
- **Deliverable sequencing** — Taxonomy and the Threat Modeling / Gap Analysis are the most near-term deliverables. The Security & Privacy Design Patterns Catalog and Security Best Practices Guide will follow and build on the gap analysis output. The Cross-WG Security & Privacy Review Checklist should be invested in early to get a v0 socialized with other WG chairs so security/privacy review is in their minds as they do their own work.
- **Design Patterns vs. Best Practices framing** — Alex described the two as a pair: the Design Patterns Catalog is the more technical "here's how to implement hooks, when to use them, anti-patterns" deliverable, while the Best Practices Guide is the higher-level "if you want to do X, here's what you should do" guidance (with the cryptography "don't roll your own" example).
- **Hooks as a focus area** — Hooks have emerged as a near-universal concern across MCP Interceptors, MCP ACS / Bar's workstream, and customer conversations. Matthew has already been contributing patches to the protocol (e.g., adding error messages for hooks). Standardizing hook behavior is one of the highest-leverage things the WG could help drive.
- **Unified industry messaging** — Alex emphasized that the WG's biggest opportunity is to position itself as a thought leader by ensuring the SP message is consistent across AAIF WGs and aligned with adjacent standards groups. Conflicting or duplicated guidance across WGs would undercut adoption.
- **Cross-WG coordination model** — Govindaraj suggested AAIF (or LF) provide dedicated cross-WG coordinators / moderators, similar to CSA's model, so chairs aren't carrying that load. Alex agreed in principle but noted there is no current bandwidth for it; the WG will continue to bootstrap cross-WG coordination via the Review Checklist deliverable and direct outreach.
- **Discord usage support** — Matthew offered to record short how-to videos on Discord usage (especially for workstream channels) since the usability isn't well-advertised; he has done this in other working groups.

## Decisions

- The volunteering survey will remain open until the end of the week (target Friday), at which point Alex will close it and begin individual outreach to confirm workstream leads/co-leads and contributors.
- Each workstream will have its own dedicated Discord channel for focused coordination.
- Workstream leaders will self-organize meeting cadence and times, with status updates given at the all-hands.
- Where multiple people volunteer for leadership of the same workstream, the WG will favor co-leads and let the volunteers self-organize the assignment.
- "News You Can Use" adopted as the standing name for the world-updates agenda item.

## Deliverables

- **Taxonomy** — Now a cross-WG AAIF initiative; SP WG volunteers will participate as contributors and bring the existing SP draft to the table. Leadership selection runs through Manik this week.
- **Agentic AI Threat Modeling Gap Analysis and Framework Design** — Most near-term WG-led deliverable; still seeking a leader. Matthew Khouzam offered to help lead.
- **Security and Privacy Design Patterns Catalog** — Sequenced after the gap analysis; will define concrete patterns (hooks, checkpoints, human-in-the-loop, etc.) and anti-patterns.
- **Agentic Security Best Practices Guide** — Pairs with the Design Patterns Catalog at a higher, more advisory level.
- **Cross-Working Group Security and Privacy Review Checklist** — Targeting an early v0 to socialize with other WG chairs; still seeking a leader.
