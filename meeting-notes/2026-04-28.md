## Topics Discussed

- **Charter Technical Committee vote status** — Alex reported that the charter was presented to the Technical Committee last week. Four approvals were received but five members did not vote, so the charter did not formally pass procedurally. Feedback in the PR was minor (e.g., David from MCP raised the "S&P" acronym as a wording nit). A GitHub outage on Monday likely impacted voting; three additional verbal approvals came in afterward. Alex confirmed in the chairs Discord that the WG will proceed as if approved given there were no substantive objections.
- **Role of the AAIF Technical Committee** — In response to Tom Sheffler's question, Alex explained the TC acts as the oversight board that must formally approve working groups before they are part of AAIF. The procedural delays were attributed to volunteer commitments and the GitHub outage rather than concerns about scope or content.
- **Cross-WG taxonomy collaboration** — Coming out of the chairs meeting, every WG identified taxonomy as a deliverable. The chairs agreed to use taxonomy as the first cross-WG collaboration to exercise the muscle of working together and avoid a painful reconciliation later. The SP WG already has a draft taxonomy extracted from prior meetings and will push it as the starting point — being early gives the group a chance to define the terms it cares most about.
- **Leadership change — new AAIF CTO** — Matt is no longer CTO of AAIF; Manik is the new CTO. Manik is based in Sydney (currently traveling in the US), which introduces time-zone challenges for meeting scheduling.
- **Meeting time and cadence** — Alex shared survey results (24 responses, still open) showing the vast majority of respondents are in US Eastern, Central, and Pacific time zones. Options discussed: alternating between a 6 am Pacific slot and a later slot (effectively reducing all-hands cadence to monthly), or running shorter, more frequent split meetings at two times on the same day. Govindaraj suggested a 4 pm Eastern / 2 pm Pacific slot but noted it excludes India and Europe. Matthew Khouzam encouraged unrepresented regions to vote in the time-zone survey. Alex flagged that some members are blocked from Discord/Slack by corporate policy (especially in fintech) and the email list will be used as a fallback for updates.
- **Shift toward async + workstream self-organization** — Alex outlined the plan to evolve the bi-weekly all-hands into a shorter checkpoint/update meeting, with workstreams self-organizing their own meeting times and most working updates moving to async channels.
- **Survey for deliverables and workstream interest** — Alex will send a Google Form this week (targeting tomorrow) to gauge interest in each deliverable, with levels of involvement (observer, contributor, leader) so workstreams can be staffed and leads/co-leads identified.
- **MCP Interceptors meeting** — Alex apologized for a prior time-zone mix-up. The MCP Interceptors meeting has been rescheduled to Thursday 2026-04-30 at 11 am Eastern / 8 am Pacific. The Interceptors proposal adds hooks into the MCP protocol (relevant for data privacy, redaction, etc.) and is an opportunity for SP WG collaboration and input into landscape and best-practices deliverables.
- **MCP Apps meeting** — A meeting on MCP Apps is scheduled for 2026-05-05 at 11 am Eastern / 8 am Pacific. MCP Apps brings UI into MCP, breaking the browser boundary and raising significant security/privacy implications worth tracking.
- **MCP server for Discord** — Alex volunteered to help build an MCP server for Discord to make it easier to track meetings and updates across the AAIF and MCP Discords. Open to collaborators (DM Alex on Discord); not formally in scope of this WG.
- **Trust Boundary and Guardrail in Multi-Agent Systems roundtable** — Junjie Bu announced an invitation-only roundtable in San Francisco on agentic privacy, with a keynote by Jason Clinton (CISO, Anthropic) followed by a structured discussion. Organized by the CEO/co-founder of Silence Laboratory. Junjie offered to share the link in chat and provide introductions for anyone interested.
- **Working style — ask forgiveness, not permission** — Matthew Khouzam encouraged Alex (and the group) to keep moving fast. Alex echoed the sentiment but noted a balance is needed within the LF process; he is confident on charter and taxonomy and ready to move full speed ahead on workstream formation.

## Decisions

- The WG will proceed as if the charter is approved despite the procedural vote shortfall, given no substantive objections.
- Taxonomy will be the first cross-WG collaboration deliverable; the SP WG's existing draft will be the starting point.
- The next meeting will be held at 6 am Pacific to include Manik and assess attendance impact; a parallel later-in-the-day session may be added.
- Bi-weekly all-hands meetings will evolve into shorter update/checkpoint meetings, with workstreams self-organizing their own meeting times and most work shifting to async channels.

## Deliverables

- **Taxonomy** — Existing SP WG draft will be used as the starting point for the cross-WG taxonomy collaboration.
